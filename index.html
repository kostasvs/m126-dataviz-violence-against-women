Being issue of critical importance nowadays as much as ever, violence against women concerns all forms of physical and sexual violence, as well as psychological abuse and controlling behaviours. Femicide is among the most horrendous forms of this major problem.

While violent crimes are of equally high significance regardless of the victim gender, violence against women is a problem that needs to be addressed as a special case due to the specific factors that lead to its occurrence. The need for strategic prevention and response is apparent from the impact on the physical, sexual and mental health of the victims, which may at many cases be fatal.

It has been 46 years since Diana Russell coined the word "Femicide" in March 1976 at the 1st international tribunal for crimes against women in Brussels. The aim of this word was to make the world aware that the violent death of women is a crime that should not be confused with the gender-neutral term "Homicide". Characteristically, she had stated that 'it is impossible to mobilize against something without a name, that is, something that until now has been "invisible". Words are what gives meaning to the world.

How many homicides take place each year across the world?

The frequency of homicides worldwide has remained roughly the same in the long term.

The victims are more often men than women.

Despite the fact that men are murdered more often than women, there are very few cases where this happens because of their gender. Femicide occurs in patriarchal societies, in which women are expected to be receptive and passive. So femicide, to give the definition, is the killing of a woman or girl mainly by a man (usually a partner) because of her gender alone. Elements such as patriarchal values can increase the risk of violence due to the belief that women can and should be controlled by men. They are exposed to all forms of violence and pressure. The right to life is violated. Its main characteristic is that it is systematic, continuous and conscious. Women face violence and are exposed to femicide around the world from childhood.

Homicides in general context are quite rare. But they are the subject of media interest and numerous works of fiction. Usually these seem to focus on cases with unusual forms of homicide such as those with a sexual or unknown motive, as well as those carried out e.g. by a serial killer. On the contrary, data shows that the majority of murders are committed by the victim's intimates or by young men in conflicts and fights.

The analysis and observation of homicide statistics must be done with a careful approach. We need to keep in mind that these data are not necessarily accurate, as each country's legal definitions of homicide may differ, resulting in challenges in the identification and recording of homicides. A typical example is Japan, which does not record homicides in a robbery as homicides.

How are homicides scattered across the world?

Between 1990 and 2020, Brazil and India noted the highest counts of homicides. While the majority of the victims in Brazil were men, most victims in India were women.

Shifting our focus to homicide counts relative to total population, El Salvador and Colombia have the highest counts. Considering male victims, Colombia is at the top, but female victims per total population are highest in Russia.

The last few years seem to follow these same trends.

Drug trafficking and gang activity is to blame for Brazil's high count of male victims. In contrast, the high femicide count in India is the result of multiple manifestations, including female infanticide, forced abortions, honor killings and dowry murders. These are a few of the various forms of femicide covering a very large percentage of murders committed by an intimate person, mainly a partner. Other notable cases include killing of women and girls during war, and killing of women due to sexual preference, decision to divorce, adultery or refusal to marry.

The high homicide rate with male victims in Colombia and El Salvador is again due to the extent of drug trafficking, gang activity and gang violence. On the other hand, Russia's high count of femicides is a complex issue and linked to high rates of domestic violence, which was decriminalized in Russia in 2017.

Apart from cases that are not recorded due to ignorance, there are cases that are wrongly recorded as deaths due to health issues or accident. Cases of femicide in the context of human trafficking, forced prostitution and female genital mutilation fall under the category of femicide. In addition, another incorrect register is the case in which a family that cannot provide food for all the children, neglects the girls resulting in malnutrition and ultimately their death.

Finally, the covid-19 pandemic has been a huge obstacle not only to data collection, but first and foremost an obstacle for many women to find safe shelter from violent partners and to access hospital facilities and services.

USA: How are crimes distributed across age and race?

While assaults and homicides peak in the age range of 20 - 40 years, sex offenses are highest in the age range of 10 - 25 years.

Assaults and homicides against Black or African American women seem to be skewed towards a younger age range than other races.

Looking into the FBI 2020 data, the incident count across the victim age range seems to follow the same trend regardless of victim race.

Regarding assault and homicide offenses, the victim ages follow a normal distribution, skewed towards the younger ages and peaking at the range of 25-30 years. However, sex offense victims are in a much younger range with the majority even being minors.

USA: Where do the crimes take place?

In all violent offense categories, the majority of the incidents happened in residential properties.

After that, roads, sidewalks, alleys and parking lots are among the most frequent scenes of crime. Hotels/motels are a common place where Sex offenses take place.

Domestic violence is a significant portion of violent incidents occurring, which is partly the reason why homes are the most prevalent scene of crime. Domestic violence, as well as any kind of violence occurring within home limits, is often difficult to perceive, identify and prevent.

Following that, most violent offenses have been recorded on roads, sidewalks, alleys. However, hotels and similar establishments were the most common place of sexual offenses after homes.

How are the offenders related to the victims?

The majority of femicides worldwide are committed by a victim's intimate partner.

In the USA in 2020, the victims' partners are likewise the most frequent femicide and assault perpetrators, but acquaintances or even strangers are also among the most frequent. Sex offenses, on the contrary, where most often carried out by acquaintances.

USA: What are the mechanisms used and the damage done?

Firearms were the most common weapon used in homicides in the USA in 2020. This also applies for assault and sex offenses.

Some form of weapon or offensive mechanism was used for 70% of sex offenses, and 90% of assaults.

Non-homicide offenses usually resulted in minor injuries.

Regarding the means used to carry out the murder, those with the highest percentages are weapons (handguns, shotguns, firearms, rifles), knives and sharp objects in general. The use of the last two should not surprise us, as they are readily available to everyone. Besides these, shootings are quite common among young men (the majority of offenders). Murders/homicides in which alcohol was involved are more likely (compared to non-alcohol-related homicides) not to involve a gun. In contrast, drug-related homicides have an increased chance of a gun being used by someone known to the victim.

It goes without saying that where firearms and other deadly weapons are available, small disputes, differences and skirmishes have a greater chance of ending in a fatal outcome. Also, while the murder weapon is not our main concern, it is important to point out the correlation between the right to bear arms and the high murder rates in the US.

To conclude, we reach 2 important “verdicts”.

  1. By informing the world about femicides the purpose is both to raise public awareness and potential victims to seek help and support. This work requires the collective participation of professionals such as teachers, health workers, members of the police force, judicial authorities, officials who know how to handle cases of gender-based violence, as well as women themselves in associations and communities.
  2. Femicide/feminicide whenever and wherever it occurs, it is vital that it be called by its name in the media and not by terms that have been heard much in the past such as "crime of passion" so that the seriousness of the crime is being felt by the public.
